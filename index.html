The House

Old Brussels, decorated by hand.

Hôtel Mozart occupies a merchant's residence from the 17th century on Rue du Marché aux Fromages — one street back from the Grand Place, close enough to hear the carillon, far enough to sleep through it.

Every room was decorated by hand, one at a time, over years — antique mirrors, hand-painted ceilings, a Moroccan-tiled courtyard built around a fountain that still runs. It's more curiosity cabinet than showroom, and that's rather the point.
